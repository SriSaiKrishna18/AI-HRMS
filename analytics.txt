Should you add graphs? Yes — but the bar is high.
Graphs that are genuinely good make the product look like a real SaaS tool. Graphs that are bad — wrong data, meaningless axes, ugly colors, no labels — make it look worse than no graphs at all. Evaluators have seen a thousand React dashboards with recharts bar charts showing fake data. That impresses nobody.
What would actually impress:
A Task Completion Trend line chart — tasks completed per day over the last 14 days. This is real data from your DB, shows time-series thinking, and is directly relevant to the productivity AI feature.
A Department Performance bar chart — average productivity score per department. This connects your AI scoring to business-level insights, which is exactly what an HRMS should show.
A Skill Coverage donut chart — across all employees, what percentage have each skill. Shows workforce capability at a glance.
A Task Status Distribution pie chart — what % are assigned vs in progress vs completed right now. Simple but gives the dashboard an at-a-glance operational view.
What would NOT impress:
Hardcoded fake data in charts. If the numbers don't come from your actual DB, an evaluator who clicks around will notice the charts never change. Instant credibility killer.
Generic recharts examples copy-pasted with placeholder labels. They can tell.
Charts that show the same information already shown in the stat cards above them. Redundant = lazy.

Honest reaction: don't use D3 for this.
D3 is the right answer if you're building custom, bespoke visualizations that can't be done any other way — force-directed graphs, geographic maps, custom SVG animations. For bar charts, line charts, and donuts on an HRMS dashboard, D3 is massive overkill and will eat 6–8 hours of your time fighting axis scales and SVG transforms instead of building something that matters.
You have limited time before the demo video. D3 will burn it.
Use Recharts instead. It's built on D3 under the hood, looks professional, takes 30 minutes to integrate, and evaluators cannot tell the difference in a demo video. The chart quality is identical visually.
